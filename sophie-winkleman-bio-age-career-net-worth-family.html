Sophie Winkleman Bio, Age, Career, Net Worth, Family

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, August 2, 2024

Sophie Lara Winkleman is an English actress who was born on August 5th, 1980 in Primrose Hill, London in the United Kingdom.

She was born to Barry Winkleman and Cindy Black. Winkleman happens to be the half-sister to the TV personality, Claudia Winkleman whom she shares the same father with.

Sophie Winkleman had her education at Trinity Hall, Cambridge, where she studied English literature, and the City of London School for Girls.

She became a member of the Cambridge Footlights and created and acted in the three-month British tour of the comedic revue Far Too Happy, which earned the group its first Perrier Award nomination in 20 years.

In 1997, she became a member of the National Youth Theatre of Great Britain. She performs her soprano voice in the First Love series for Sky Arts.

Sophie Winkleman career

Winkleman’s credits include recurring roles in series including Big Suze in Peep Show, numerous roles in Harry & Paul, Joely in White Teeth, Fiona in The Trial of Tony Blair, Abby in Plus One, Katerina in Red Dwarf: Back to Earth, Donna in Lead Balloon, Prudence in Keen Eddie, Elle Kensington in Chasing Alice, Regan Peverill in the pilot episode of Lewis.

Winkleman also played Angela Warren in Agatha Christie’s Poirot (in the episode “Five Little Pigs”), Princess Eleanor in The Palace, Ghislaine in Robin Hood, Alice Shadwell in Dalziel and Pascoe, Ann Hamilton in Death in Paradise, Jill in TV Land’s Hot in Cleveland, Sharon Kirby in CSI Miami and Dorothy Gibson in Titanic.

Winkleman’s portrayal of Clara Gold in Waking the Dead earned her a BBC nomination for Best Newcomer. She made her breakthrough on American television as the lead in the NBC comedy 100 Questions as Charlotte Payne.

She also had several appearances as a guest star on the popular series Two and a Half Men as Zoey, Walden Schmidt’s (Ashton Kutcher) British girlfriend.

Alongside Eddie Izzard, Russell Branson, and Billy Connolly, she portrayed Helena in Eric Idle’s musical What About Dick at the Orpheum Theatre in downtown Los Angeles in 2012.

The lead roles in the Mike Figgis-written and -directed movies Shattered and Love Live Long are among Winkleman’s filmography credits.

Additionally, Winkleman portrayed the adult Susan Pevensie in the Disney movie The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe as well as the comedic part of Debbie Levine in the romantic comedy Suzie Gold from Pathé.

Other film roles include the leads in Debs Gardner-Post Paterson’s and the shorts Seared, Love Letters, The Lost Domain, which is a film adaptation of Alain-Le Fournier’s Grand Meaulnes.

Winkleman is a recurring character on BBC Radio 4 drama and comedy. She is a member of the cast of afternoon plays like Tea for Two as well as comedic shows like Marcus Brigstocke’s Giles Wemmbley-Hogg Goes Off.

For Radio 4, she appeared as Polly Pot in P.G. Wodehouse’s Uncle Fred in the Springtime alongside Alfred Molina and Rufus Sewell, Gloria in Bernard Shaw’s You Never Can Tell, and Zoe with Jared Harris in Alan Ayckbourn’s Henceforward.

She also portrayed Sasha in the historical drama Von Ribbentrop’s Watch, Anna Freud in the Laurence Marks and Maurice Gran play Dr. Freud Will See You Now, Mrs. Hitler, and Penthesilea, an Amazon warrior princess, with Alistair McGowan and Stephen Mangan in the Radio 4 comic fantasy series ElvenQuest. She has additionally appeared in various Doctor Who radio plays.

On December 3rd, 2017, it was revealed that Winkleman had suffered a broken back and foot as a result of a head-on collision.

She was riding in the backseat of a car driven by a chauffeur when another motorist struck her car after swerving to miss a deer. She will not experience long-term issues, but weeks of recovery were expected.
